About Me

I am Mengji Zhang, a Postdoc Associate at Yale University as of June 2024. My research focuses on computational biology. My long-term research goal is to advance the decoding of biological problems with computational algorithms.

I received my PhD supervised by Prof. Kun Qian in Shanghai Jiao Tong University with works about computational metabolomics in June, 2023. I developed the first deep-learning algorithm to stabilize the measured metabolic profiles for improved diagnostic reproducibility, together with diagnostic variational surface to measure diagnostic accuracy and variation simultaneously.

I collaborated with Prof. Tetsuya Kobayashi from Tokyo University on decoding olfactory perception from molecular structures. This work builds a deep graph model with atomic position encoding and Coulomb matrix to predict the molecular properties. It may promote the understanding and decoding of the olfactory sense and mechanisms.

I collaborated with Prof. Tian Xu and Prof. Dong Yang from Westlake University on integrating the large and heterogeneous transcriptome datasets. This work builds a deep generative model with adversarial learning and contrastive learning to remove various unwanted variations from transcriptomic studies. It robustly correct diverse undesirable variations and accurately preserve biological signals for batch, platform, purity and unknown variations.

Now I am collaborating with Prof. Hyojung Seo from Yale University on decoding the interaction between behaviors and neural activities in the brain with deep learning.

If you are interested in collaboration with me about computational metabolomics/transcriptomics and neuroscience, please contact mengji.zhang0809@gmail.com.
